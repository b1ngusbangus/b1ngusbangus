  • What is something you learned from this article? (3-4 sentences)

    From this article, I learned how quickly our brains perceive images and objects based on four key ideas: emergence, reification, multi-stability and invariance. These four ideas accumulate on top of each other, more or less: with emergence, our brains will first recognize some kind of general outline of the object/image; subsequently, reification will make what we think we're perceiving more concrete as our brains will naturally pattern-match and make similar associations based on accumulated memory. With multi-stability, our brains will essentially bounce back and forth with more ambiguous-looking objects/images as we are able to perceive the same thing in multiple ways which could trip people up as people become more accustomed to seeing one image versus the other(s). Finally, invariance allows our brains to identify objects regardless of what varying shape or form they can take on (i.e. a 2-d vs 3-d die: we still can tell it's a die regardless of its dimensionality).
